The Journey / Design Process

Reaching this final product wasn't easy requiring a lot of thinking and effort. I found myself cycling many times through the engineering design proecess especially the prototyping, testing and improving processes as I faced many obstacles. Some of those challenges included: the design exceeding size constraints, assembly being too difficult, certain parts not functioning as planned, absence of fllexible filament etc. To resolve these issues, I tackled each problem individually and asked parents and siblings for feedback until I finally reached this final design.

The most enjoyable moment of this entire project was assmbling the 3D printed parts and seeing my creation come to life. Seeing all the pieces come perfectly together feels very rewarding after all the effort that was put into it (Question 2.4).
I realized about myself that I'm not as bad as I thought I was when it came to the aesthetic aspect of the design. My parents ans iblings complimented the design's aesthetic saying it looked very good. I was also very satisfied with how it loooked especially the use of gold and black (Question 3.2).
This design has an ancient Egyptian theme. It has hidden symbolism and a secret message in hieroglyphs that reads, "Live life to the fullest." The Ankh symbolizes life, death and reincarnation which is very fitting for a piece of jewelry that has multiple forms (Question 4.1).
One thing I would change about this design if I were given more time is to make it even more versatile that it already is. The pendant is versatile enough, however, the string or chain is very static. Making the chain / string composed of multiple small sections could give this jewelry a new form like a watch or a ring on top of everything else it already has (Question 5.3).

Pendant Variation 1

This varitation of the pendant mode is the result of all the parts assembled together. This is the original form of this design and it takes the shape of an Ankh. The hieroglyph engravings on it read, "Live life to the fullest". To connect it to the chain or string, one can simply screw it to the hole on top by rotating the Ankh until it is tight.

Pendant Variation 2

The second variation of the pendant mode is the top curved part of the Ankh being disassembled and used a long as a pendant. It is smaller and more compact for those who want a more minimilistic look. To connect it to the chain or string, you can simply screw it to the hole on either side of the curved part.

Pendant Variation 3

The final variation of this pendant is similar to the second variation. Instead of using the curved part, you use the lower part of the Ankh as a standalone pendant. It has a small sotrage area at the back with a magnetic cover for easy access. To connect it to the chain or string, you can simply screw it to the hole on top.

Assemble Earings Step 1

Unscrew the screw connecting all the Ankh pieces, be sure not to lose the nut. Remove the magnetic cover and put the screw and nut into the storage area of the lower piece. Put the cover back and make sure it is sealed properly.

Assemble Earings Step 2

Take the 2 side pieces and hold each of the side pieces' halves. Slide them in a way so that the longer half is pushed to the direction of the hole on it. Once this is done, isolate the longer pieces. Put the earing hooks somewhere nearby for easy access.

Assemble Earings Step 3

At the end of each hook, there will be a smaller hook. Open the hooks and carefully clip them onto the longer halves of the side pieces so that the side with the hieroglyph engravings is facing away from the tip of the bigger hook. Make sure they are connected properly.
